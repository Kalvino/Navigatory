USEFUL INFORMATION

1. What is the ideal duration of a trip in West USA?

- Itineraries can range from one week to more than a month. If you only want to stay on the Californian Coast, one week is enough but to get a good overview of the region, you'll need at least 2 weeks; 3 weeks are ideal.

- Do not forget to consider the time zone and jetlag. When arriving from continental Europe to California, you'll "gain" 9 hours that you will "loose" when you come back. This is why planes usually leave in the afternoon and only arrives that day after at your destination.

- Be careful at the distances! Do not undermine them! You can have a detailed look at them in our itinerary planning tool. In needed, lower the number of sites you plan to visit. Try to have an average travelling time of 3.5 hours. Of course, it's up to you to define your rythm!

2. What is the best season to travel in West USA?

- You can visit the Region of West USA all year long. Each season offers its benefits and inconvenient. Most travelers decide to travel somewhere between beginning of April and end of March.

- During the Summer, some sites are relatively crowded and it is required to book your hotels in advance. Temperature are higher, which is good in some places (like San Francisco or Yosemite) but other places like the Desert and Las Vegas will be too hot.

- During the Spring and Fall, sites are less crowded and hence prices are generally lower. Though, it is advised to book your hotels in advance, especially if you want to stay in the national parks. Temperatures can be relatively cold in altitude (for example at Bryce Canyon).

- During the Winter, most national parks are covered by snow. It's then an entirely different scenery that you will observe. Pay attention, if you plan to go to Yosemite during the Winter, you will not be able to use the East entrance because the Tioga Pass Road will be closed.
